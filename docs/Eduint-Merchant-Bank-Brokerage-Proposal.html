04 · Problem Statement

What's hurting your firm today

Through conversations with merchant bank and brokerage leadership across Dhaka and Chattogram, we have consistently heard the same three pain points:

1. Client experience gap

Retail investors expect the same quality of app they get from Nagad, bKash, or Rocket. Most firms offer a Windows-only thick client with no learning, no demo, no portfolio analysis. The churn rate on dormant BO accounts is 40%+ in year two.

2. Compliance is reactive

Monthly BSEC audit is a scramble of Excel files, PDFs, and phone calls. Margin breaches, short-sale violations, and KYC staleness are caught after they become findings — not before.

3. Operations are manual

EOD, fund accounting, custodian ops, and reconciliation are still operated on spreadsheets. A single settlement error can wipe out a month of brokerage margin.

4. In-house build is too slow

Building a modern investor platform requires 8–12 engineers, 18+ months, and a product team that most firms do not have. DIY projects typically stall around the EOD or KYC module.

05 · Case Study

How Eduint transformed Ekush Investments

Ekush Investments is a Bangladeshi asset management company that manages three unit funds — EFUF (Ekush First Unit Fund), EGF (Ekush Growth Fund), and ESRF (Ekush Shariah Return Fund). Until 2025, Ekush — like most AMCs in Bangladesh — ran its entire operation on spreadsheets.

Before Eduint — the "Excel AMC"

How things worked

  • NAV calculated manually in Excel by the fund accountant, once a day.
  • Three separate workbooks per fund — FIN STATS.xlsx and INVESTORS.xlsx.
  • Unit certificates printed on pre-stamped paper and couriered.
  • Tax certificates & dividend warrants posted via Bangladesh Post.
  • Investor reporting: once-a-year factsheet PDF, printed and mailed.
  • SIP handled by bank standing order; collections reconciled by phone.
  • Investor complaints routed through the RM's personal WhatsApp.

What it cost

  • ~6 people-days / month spent on NAV-to-investor pack.
  • 14-day delay between NAV publication and investor visibility.
  • ~3% SIP attrition driven by delayed acknowledgements.
  • Audit findings on manual journal entries and unreconciled subscriptions.
  • Zero digital touchpoints with investors — a retention liability.

The Eduint engagement

In 8 weeks, Eduint built and rolled out a full digital AMC stack, branded for Ekush, covering all three funds (EFUF / EGF / ESRF) — admin console, investor portal, and compliance engine — on a modern cloud-native foundation.

10 · The Monthly Audit Deliverable

What "Monthly Audit" actually means

On the first business day of every month, Eduint delivers a signed, board-ready PDF covering every regulated surface of your firm — pre-mapped to BSEC and DSE guidelines.

Sections of the monthly report

Section What it contains
Executive summary Traffic-light status across KYC, margin, settlement, complaints & cyber hygiene.
KYC & onboarding Stale KYC count, expired IDs, pending document cases, re-KYC SLA adherence.
Trade surveillance Margin breaches, short-sale exceptions, wash-trade candidates, crossed-market flags.
Settlement & reconciliation T+1/T+2 settlement success rate, failed settlements, broken reconciliations.
Client complaints & NPS Complaints opened/closed, median-time-to-resolution, NPS pulse from the portal.
BSEC rule alignment Line-by-line mapping of trades, disclosures, and processes to BSEC rule IDs.
IT & cyber hygiene Auth logs, admin-action trail, failed-login anomalies, backup integrity checks.
Findings & remediation Open findings, target close-dates, owners, and links to tickets.

How the audit is produced

  1. Continuously collected — every event (trade, KYC update, margin call, reconciliation break) is logged into a structured audit trail as it happens.
  2. Auto-aggregated — on the 1st of the month, the rule engine runs over the prior 30 days of data and classifies findings.
  3. Eduint reviewed — a senior analyst spot-checks the auto-findings, adds narrative, and signs the PDF.
  4. Delivered — PDF dropped into your compliance email alias and SharePoint/Drive within 3 business days.
Board-ready. The audit PDF is formatted to be dropped directly into your next board pack — no reformatting required.
